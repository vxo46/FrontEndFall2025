Same Walls, Different Centuries

A quick comparison of intent, tools, and audience.

Intent

Cave Art

Ritual, teaching, or storytelling about animals and people.

Street Art

Identity, protest, and public dialogue in cities.

Materials

Cave: ochre, charcoal, clay; brushes, fingers, air-spray through reeds.

Street: spray paint, rollers, pastes, stencils, chalk.

Audience

Cave: clan/community in restricted spaces.

Street: the public — commuters, neighbors, tourists.

Origins & Examples (Tabs)

Switch between ancient caves and modern street art.

  • El Castillo (Spain, >40k years): red hand stencils & disks made by blowing pigment over hands.
  • Chauvet (France, ~36k years): shaded horses, lions, and rhinos showing motion.
  • Sulawesi (Indonesia, ~45k years): red-ochre wild pigs and handprints; oldest figurative art.
  • Keith Haring – Subway Drawings (1980s): chalk on black subway panels; public, accessible symbols.
  • Keith Haring – Crack Is Wack (1986): handball court mural protesting the crack epidemic.
  • Banksy – Cave Painting Removal (2008): worker power-washing over prehistoric animals and handprints.
